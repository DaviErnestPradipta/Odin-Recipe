Sambal

Description

Sambal is an Indonesian chili sauce or paste, typically made from a mixture of chillis with secondary ingredients such as shrimp paste, garlic, ginger, shallot, scallion, palm sugar, and lime juice. Sambal is an Indonesian loanword of Javanese origin (sambel). In addition to Indonesian cuisine, sambal is also an integral part of the cuisines of Singapore, Malaysia, Brunei, and Sri Lanka. It has also spread through overseas Indonesian populations to the Netherlands and Suriname. Different sambal recipes are served as hot and spicy condiments for dishes, such as lalab (raw vegetables), ikan bakar (grilled fish), ikan goreng (fried fish), ayam goreng (fried chicken), ayam penyet (smashed chicken), iga penyet (ribs), and various soto soups. There are at least 212 variants of sambal in Indonesia, most of which originate in Java

Ingredients

  • 100 grams of chili pepper
  • 400 grams of tomato sauce
  • 0.5 tablespoon of white sugar
  • 0.5 tablespoon of MSG
  • 0.5 tablespoon of salt
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of cooking oil

Steps

  1. Blend all ingredients except cooking oil until they are smooth
  2. Heat up the cooking oil on a wide pan
  3. Cook the blended ingredients and stir constantly
  4. Wait until the breaking oil—where the sambal will likely bubble and explode—ends
  5. Put into a container and let it cool down to room temperature, then store in the fridge
